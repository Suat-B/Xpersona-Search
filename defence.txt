Binary Defence Roadmap
Operator Copilot capabilities for defense-support, cyber, sustainment, and mission-adjacent workflows

Binary is being positioned as a local-first, auditable operator copilot for analysts, maintainers, cyber teams, planners, and operators. The product is meant to help people do difficult operational work with more speed, trust, and clarity. It does not target weapons, targeting, lethality, or offensive harm workflows.

Safety Boundary

In scope:
- cyber defense
- operator assistance
- documentation
- logistics
- maintenance
- readiness
- compliance
- training
- secure automation

Out of scope:
- weapons control
- targeting
- autonomous lethality
- guidance
- offensive kill-chain automation

High-risk actions remain approval-gated with human-in-the-loop oversight.

Priority Workflow Packs

- Cyber incident triage: collect signals, structure the case, and guide next actions across Website, Binary IDE, and Cutie.
- Alert enrichment and evidence collection: gather console, browser, document, and terminal evidence across Binary IDE and Cutie.
- Maintenance troubleshooting: walk technicians through procedures, evidence capture, and repair notes in Binary IDE and Website.
- Readiness checklist execution: run repeatable checklists and verification flows across Website and Binary IDE.
- Document and report drafting: turn evidence into clear drafts and operator summaries across Binary IDE, Cutie, and Website.
- Ticket and portal operations: assist with structured browser workflows and updates in Binary IDE and Website.
- Secure research and document extraction: pull facts from trusted sources and working documents across Website, Binary IDE, and Cutie.
- Training and after-action review: support guided learning, replay, and debrief workflows across Website and Binary IDE.
- Compliance evidence gathering: assemble proofs, receipts, and validation artifacts across Website, Binary IDE, and Cutie.
- Program status and operations summary generation: produce leadership-ready updates from live work artifacts across all three surfaces.
